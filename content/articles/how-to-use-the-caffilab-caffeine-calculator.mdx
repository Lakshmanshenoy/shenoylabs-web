## What is CaffiLab?

CaffiLab is a scientific caffeine estimation tool that calculates how much caffeine is in your coffee based on your exact brew parameters — not generic averages from a database. It uses a model grounded in extraction science:

**C = dose × bean caffeine range midpoint × recovery**

Instead of telling you "a cup of coffee has 95 mg," CaffiLab accounts for your specific coffee dose, bean species, brew method, grind size, water temperature, brew time, and more.

## Why different methods extract differently

CaffiLab doesn't use one universal formula. It assigns each brew method to a **physics class** and uses a sub-model appropriate for that class:

- **Pressure methods** (espresso): pressure differential drives fast extraction through a compacted bed. Grind is the dominant variable; ratio has a muted effect because wider pulls mainly dilute concentration rather than increase recovery percentage.
- **Percolation methods** (pour over, Chemex, drip machine, siphon, percolator): water passes through the bed under gravity, vacuum drawdown, or recirculation. Ratio matters more than for pressure — more water through the same bed genuinely extracts a higher percentage of caffeine.
- **Hot immersion methods** (French press, Turkish, immersion dripper): coffee steeps until separation. Time follows an exponential saturation curve, not a linear ramp. Turkish coffee is classified as immersion because it is a full-immersion method with no filtration — its ultra-fine grind and near-boiling temperature are already encoded in its high base recovery.
- **Cold brew**: extraction rate scales with temperature following the Arrhenius relationship. At fridge temperature (4 °C) the effective time constant is about 11 hours — meaning a 12-hour fridge brew only reaches about 65 % of the 0.88 equilibrium ceiling. At room temperature (22 °C) the time constant drops to ~7 hours and the same 12-hour steep reaches ~85 % of ceiling.
- **Hybrid methods** (AeroPress, Moka pot, Indian filter): each combines two extraction mechanisms. AeroPress uses an immersion steep with a manual press; Moka pot uses steam pressure through a compacted basket; Indian filter uses gravity plus mild steam assistance with long contact time. All three carry a +5 % base uncertainty penalty reflecting their higher recipe variability.
- **Cold drip**: like cold brew but single-pass percolation — equilibrium is never reached, so the ceiling is lower.

## Getting started: the basic inputs

When you open CaffiLab, you'll see the core inputs that drive the estimate:

### 1. Brew method

Select your brewing method. This sets sensible defaults for grind size, brew time, temperature, and extraction expectations, and also selects the appropriate physics class for the recovery model.

### 2. Bean species

Choose between Arabica, Robusta, Blend, or "Not sure." Robusta usually carries a meaningfully higher caffeine range than Arabica, so this choice has a large impact on the estimate.

If you select "Not sure," CaffiLab will use package clues and price to infer a likely blend profile.

### 3. Bean detail

Use the optional **Bean detail** control if you know more about the bean than just the species:

- **Generic range** keeps the standard species range.
- **High-altitude** biases toward the lower-caffeine side of that range.
- **Low-altitude** biases toward the higher-caffeine side.
- **Custom caffeine %** lets you enter a measured or published dry-weight caffeine percentage.

This does not add fake precision. It simply tells CaffiLab which bean range is most appropriate.

### 4. Coffee dose

Enter the weight of dry coffee grounds you're using. More coffee means more available caffeine — this is a direct multiplier in the formula.

### 5. Brew water

The volume of water you're brewing with. This determines your coffee-to-water ratio, which affects extraction efficiency. CaffiLab shows your current ratio and the method's target ratio.

### 6. Beverage volume

The final volume of liquid in your cup, including any dilution. This affects concentration (mg per 100 ml) but not total caffeine.

### 7. Brew time

How long water is in contact with the coffee grounds. The effect depends heavily on the physics class:

- For **hot immersion** (French press, Turkish), extraction saturates after about 8 minutes. Going to 20 minutes adds very little.
- For **cold brew**, time matters enormously — the difference between 6 h and 18 h at fridge temperature is much larger than the equivalent time range for a hot method.
- For **pressure methods** (espresso), brew time affects but doesn't dominate — grind size and pressure are the primary controls.

## Understanding the results

CaffiLab shows four key outputs:

- **Estimated caffeine (mg):** The headline number — total milligrams of caffeine in your cup.
- **Confidence label:** Very High, High, Medium, or Low — based on both brewing uncertainty and the bean-variability floor.
- **Precision bar:** A visual gauge showing how tight the estimate is.
- **Practical range:** The lower and upper bounds after the final uncertainty cap is applied.

You will also see the uncertainty sources broken out:

- **Bean variability:** the uncertainty caused by the bean's caffeine range itself.
- **Brewing uncertainty:** the uncertainty caused by missing or approximate brew inputs.
- **Applied cap:** the larger of those two values. Confidence cannot outrun bean variability.

## Fine-tuning with advanced inputs

Click **Advanced inputs** to reveal additional parameters:

- **Grind size:** Finer grinds expose more surface area, increasing extraction.
- **Roast level:** Light roasts can yield slightly more caffeine in comparable brews than very dark roasts.
- **Temperature:** Higher temperatures increase extraction rate significantly. For cold brew, entering your actual brew temperature (fridge vs room temp) changes the model's time constant.
- **Bean detail / custom caffeine %:** Refines the bean caffeine range if you know more than the species label.
- **Extraction yield:** If you measure TDS (total dissolved solids), entering yield is one of the strongest ways to calibrate the estimate.
- **Pressure:** Only for espresso — 9 bar is the reference point.
- **Water minerals and pH:** Balanced mineral water is closest to specialty brewing standards.
- **Bean freshness:** Very fresh beans can resist even extraction; stale beans extract less predictably.
- **Filter type:** Paper filters produce a slightly cleaner brew; metal filters retain more material.
- **Chicory:** For Indian filter coffee — chicory adds body but no caffeine.

Each brewing input you declare can reduce brewing uncertainty. But if the bean itself still has a wide caffeine range, CaffiLab keeps that uncertainty visible instead of hiding it.

**Note on cold brew temperature:** If you don't enter a brew temperature, CaffiLab assumes room temperature (22 °C). If you brew in the fridge (4 °C), entering that temperature significantly changes the estimate — fridge cold brew needs ~40 % more time to reach the same extraction as room-temperature cold brew.

## Going deeper with Expert inputs

Click **Expert inputs** for three additional bean-quality parameters that further narrow the caffeine fraction range:

- **Arabica grade:** Splits the arabica range into Specialty (1.2–1.6 % dry weight) and Commercial (1.0–1.2 %). Most specialty-roaster bags are Specialty grade.
- **Growing elevation:** High-altitude farms (above ~1500 m) correlate with slightly elevated caffeine through UV exposure and pest pressure. Low-altitude farms sit toward the lower end.
- **Extraction quality (espresso only):** Accounts for channeling and puck preparation. A poorly tamped puck can meaningfully reduce caffeine extraction compared to a well-prepared shot.
- **Cultivar:** Named arabica cultivar shifts the estimated bean caffeine fraction based on published measurements:
  - **Geisha** (~0.9–1.1 % dry weight) — distinctly lower than typical arabica.
  - **SL28** (~1.3–1.7 %) — Kenyan variety with elevated caffeine.
  - **Caturra** (~1.0–1.3 %) — Bourbon-derived, mid-range arabica.
  - **Catimor** (~1.5–2.0 %) — Robusta hybrid, sits notably higher.
  - **Unknown** is the default if you are not sure which variety you have.

These Expert inputs do not change the brewing recovery model — they refine the starting caffeine fraction in the bean before any extraction calculation.

## Tips for accurate estimates

1. **Weigh your coffee.** Scoops vary; a kitchen scale makes the dose precise.
2. **Measure your water.** Know how much water goes in and how much beverage comes out.
3. **Time your brew.** Especially for immersion methods like French press and cold brew.
4. **For cold brew, enter the actual brew temperature.** The difference between 4 °C and 22 °C substantially changes the extraction rate calculation.
5. **Start with defaults.** CaffiLab auto-fills sensible values. Adjust only what you know.
6. **Use Bean detail only when you truly know it.** Generic is the right choice unless you have a good reason to narrow the range.
7. **Check the explanation panel.** As you interact with each field, CaffiLab explains what it does and how it affects the estimate.

## Interactive features

CaffiLab includes several interactive panels designed to help you understand why the number changes as you adjust inputs.

### Extraction profile

Every brew method displays an **Extraction profile** chart immediately below the main stats. The curve shows the shape of caffeine extraction over time for that method's physics family:

- **Pressure-driven** (espresso): a sharp spike then a plateau — fast extraction at high pressure.
- **Percolation** (pour over, Chemex, drip): a smooth rise to a stable plateau as water passes through the bed.
- **Immersion** (French press, Turkish): an S-curve rising toward saturation — time matters but diminishing returns set in.
- **Cold immersion** (cold brew): a very gradual climb over many hours reflecting Arrhenius-limited diffusion.
- **Cold drip**: a steady rise that never fully plateaus because single-pass contact time is limited.
- **Hybrid** (AeroPress, Moka pot, Indian filter): a combined shape reflecting the dual extraction mechanism.

A gold **"Your brew" indicator** moves along the curve as you change inputs, showing where your current extraction recovery falls on the time axis. Watch it shift left or right as you adjust brew time, temperature, or grind.

### Method hint

When you select a brew method, a short description appears below the selector summarising its extraction character — for example, "High pressure · Fast extraction · Incomplete recovery" for espresso or "Low temperature · Time-driven equilibrium · Slow diffusion" for cold brew.

### How was this calculated?

Below the uncertainty cards in the result panel, tap **How was this calculated?** to expand a visual breakdown of the three factors driving the headline number:

- **Dose (G):** your coffee mass in grams.
- **Bean strength (F_mid):** the effective caffeine fraction for the declared or inferred species.
- **Extraction (E):** the caffeine recovery percentage for this method and inputs.

Each factor is shown as a proportional bar so you can see at a glance whether dose, bean choice, or extraction efficiency is contributing most to the result.

### Confidence range bar

The precision bar below the headline estimate is a colour-coded range visualiser. It shows:

- The **practical range** (min – max) as a coloured band
- A **marker line** at the estimated value
- **Colour coding** based on how wide the uncertainty is: green for a narrow range, gold for moderate, orange for wide

The bean-driven range is shown in small text below — a useful reference for how much of the uncertainty comes from the bean itself rather than your brew inputs.

### What changed

Below the confidence bar, a **What changed** list appears as soon as you start adjusting inputs. Each row shows which field you just changed and how much it moved the estimate — for example, "Dose +22 mg" or "Grind size -4 mg". The list keeps the last four changes so you can trace the effect of a series of adjustments without losing track.

The delta badge next to the headline number ("+18" in green or "-12" in red) always reflects the most recent single change.

### Advanced science section

Below Expert inputs, an **Advanced science** section (collapsed by default) contains explanations of the three core model components for users who want to understand the mechanics:

- **Semi-mechanistic model:** how physics-informed extraction recovery and empirically calibrated secondary adjustments combine.
- **Arrhenius cold brew model:** the temperature-dependent diffusivity equation governing cold brew and cold drip extraction.
- **Uncertainty model:** how bean variability and brewing uncertainty combine into the final confidence cap.

## A note on accuracy

CaffiLab is an estimation tool, not a laboratory instrument. Actual caffeine content varies with cultivar, altitude, growing conditions, roast batch, water chemistry, and brewing geometry. The model is calibrated against published extraction research and aims to give you a useful, directionally accurate number — not a precise chemical measurement.

For most practical purposes — understanding whether you're having 100 mg or 300 mg, comparing brew methods, or tracking daily intake — CaffiLab gives you a much better answer than generic tables.
