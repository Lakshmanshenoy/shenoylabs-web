# How much caffeine is in cold brew?

This article summarizes measured caffeine concentrations in cold brew, explains typical sources of variation, and shows how to interpret results when using the Scientific Caffeine Calculator.

## Quick summary

- Typical measured concentrations for cold brew (mg per 100 mL):
  - Low: ~25–50 mg/100mL
  - Typical: ~60–120 mg/100mL
  - Strong: 150+ mg/100mL
- Wide variation occurs due to brew ratio, extraction time, grind size, and coffee origin.

## Why this matters

Knowing approximate concentration helps users translate volume → milligrams of caffeine, which is the input the calculator expects. The calculator combines a measured or estimated concentration with serving size and user-specific factors to provide actionable guidance.

## Example (practical)

If your cold brew measures ~100 mg/100mL and you drink 300 mL, your intake is ~300 mg of caffeine — roughly equivalent to 3 standard cups of coffee.
